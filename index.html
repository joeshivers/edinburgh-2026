Scotland · Final Leg · 7–11 July 2026

Edinburgh
on foot

Three walking days out of a flat in Leith. Bookshops, one arthouse screen, queer pubs not clubs, and a 25,000-step floor. Tourist things done early, local things done properly.

Tue 07 · Leith → New Town Wed 08 · Old Town + Cameo Thu 09 · Summit → Sea
Day 01 — Tue 07 Jul

Arrival loop: New Town, Stockbridge, the Walk home

In from Dundee, early. Bag → Waverley left luggage (nr. platform 2) — or ask Lucy about an early drop. Target: 25k+

  • morning

    Waverley → New Town

    Stash the bag, coffee, then north down the Georgian grid via Dundas Street's galleries.

  • late morning

    Stockbridge books

    Golden Hare Books, then the famously good charity shops.

  • midday

    Dean Village via Water of Leith

    Fifteen minutes upstream on the river path. Go before the tour groups thicken.

  • afternoon

    Royal Botanic Garden

    Free. A slow hour. Best skyline view of the castle from the lawns.

  • late afternoon

    Broughton Street — Pink Triangle queer

    First pint at The Street or Planet. Bars, not clubs. Funnels naturally back toward Waverley.

  • ~5pm

    Collect bag → Leith Walk

    25 minutes downhill to the flat. McNaughtan's Bookshop is directly on the route — Scotland's oldest antiquarian dealer, basically the local.

  • evening

    The Shore, Leith

    Pints at Roseleaf or the King's Wark. If energy holds: counter seat at Eleanore (book).

Day 02 — Wed 08 Jul

Old Town early, books at West Port, film at the Cameo

Tourist hours beaten by starting at the top of the Walk. Target: 25k+

  • before 9

    Calton Hill

    Twenty-five minutes from the flat, twenty up. The best view in the city, before the crowds.

  • morning

    Royal Mile → Victoria Street → Grassmarket

    The honest tourist sweep, done early enough to enjoy it.

  • midday

    West Port bookshops books

    Armchair Books — chaotic, towering, correct — then Edinburgh Books next door.

  • rain option

    National Museum of Scotland

    Free, vast, five minutes from the Grassmarket.

  • ~6:30pm

    Dinner in Tollcross

    Ting Thai Caravan (counter Thai) or Kim's Mini Meals (tiny Korean, cult, cash).

  • 8:15pm

    The Cameo — The Furious to book

    1914 auditorium, proper bar for the pre-film pint. Book at picturehouses.com. Fallback: Filmhouse programme on Lothian Road, 10 min away.

  • night

    Walk home via Broughton

    Optional Pink Triangle nightcap; otherwise a bus down the Walk is honourable.

Day 03 — Thu 09 Jul

Arthur's Seat to the sea, and a Leith finale

The trip's summit day. Gentle line up and down — left side gets a vote. Target: 30k

  • morning

    Arthur's Seat via Dunsapie Loch

    The gradual route both directions; skip the Gutted Haddie scramble. Short summit push, whole city and firth below.

  • midday

    Duddingston — Sheep Heid Inn

    Descend to the village. Arguably Scotland's oldest pub; a half at minimum is compulsory.

  • afternoon

    Portobello

    Walk east to the beach suburb. Promenade, coffee, sea air.

  • late afternoon

    The Regent, Abbeyhill queer

    The queer pub done right — cask ale, dogs, zero club energy.

  • evening

    Last supper in Leith

    Eleanore (counter, natural wine, small plates) or The Walnut. Both tiny — book ahead.
