edinburgh, on foot

tue 07 — thu 09 july 2026. base: a flat in leith, off the walk.

tue 07 — new town, stockbridge, the walk home

in from dundee. bag → waverley left luggage, near platform 2. order below is the walking order.

route on google maps ↗

the milkman [coffee] →
specialty coffee shop in a 17th-century shopfront on cockburn street, two minutes from waverley.
dundas street galleries [art] →
a strip of commercial art galleries along the georgian new town — the scottish gallery and open eye are the anchors. open from 10am, free to browse.
shelter bookshop, stockbridge [used books] →
charity bookshop on raeburn place with a deep, well-sorted secondhand stock. several other strong charity shops sit on the same street.
kay's bar [pub] →
tiny former wine merchant's on india street — coal fire, cask ale, red-painted walls, regulars.
evening option: the last word saloon — candlelit basement on st stephen street with a serious classic-cocktail list. →
the stockbridge tap [lunch · pub] →
cask free house with a proper kitchen — pies, fish and chips, rotating scottish ales.
alt: the cumberland bar — new town local with a leafy beer garden and honest pub lunches. →
alt: herbie of edinburgh — old-school deli counter doing sandwiches and salads to take away. →
artisan roast, stockbridge [coffee] →
the roastery's stockbridge shop on raeburn place — flat whites and beans roasted in-house.
alt: söderberg — swedish bakery, coffee and cardamom buns. →
dean village [walk] →
former milling village on the water of leith, fifteen minutes upstream on the river path — sandstone, turrets, and the well court courtyard.
alt: royal botanic garden — seventy acres of gardens and glasshouses, free, a mile north. →
scottish national portrait gallery [museum] →
neo-gothic red sandstone hall on queen street — scottish history told through portraits, from mary queen of scots to the present. free.
collect bag — waverley left luggage [logistics] →
eight minutes from the portrait gallery, near platform 2. confirm closing time at drop-off.
the street [queer] →
corner bar at the top of leith walk, in the pink triangle — broughton's historic gay quarter.
alt: planet — a block away on baxter's place. →
mcnaughtan's bookshop [used books] →
scotland's oldest antiquarian bookshop, trading since 1957, at the top of leith walk — scottish history, literature, and fine bindings.
check in — the flat [logistics]
from 3pm. with the bag: tram from st andrew square (newhaven direction) to balfour street, then a short walk — or 25 minutes on foot down the walk. the tram also stops at picardy place, outside the street.
elvis shakespeare [used books · records] →
secondhand books and vinyl at 347 leith walk — punk singles to penguin classics.
mo's sharp style [barber] →
barbershop at 280 leith walk. walk-ins, open till 7, strong reputation for beard work.
alt: blank. barbershop — two-chair shop in stockbridge; book ahead. →
nauticus [martini] →
neighbourhood cocktail bar on duke street with a maritime-leith theme — classic cocktails, seats at the bar.
fishers leith [dinner] →
seafood restaurant in a 17th-century windmill tower on the shore, open since 1991 — oysters, langoustines, and a bar to eat at.
alt: the king's wark — 500-year-old stone pub on the water with a well-regarded kitchen. →
port o' leith bar [pub] →
leith institution on constitution street — nautical flags, a jukebox, and decades of dockside history.
alt: roseleaf — snug corner bar serving cocktails in teapots. →

wed 08 — arthur's seat to the sea, leith finale

summit via the gradual dunsapie loch line, both directions.

route on google maps ↗

williams & johnson [coffee] →
leith roastery and café in custom lane, just off the shore.
alt: twelve triangles, duke street — the bakery's leith shop. →
arthur's seat via dunsapie loch [hike] →
the 251m volcanic summit of holyrood park. the path from dunsapie loch is the gradual line up — short push at the top, then the whole city and firth below.
sheep heid inn, duddingston [lunch] →
pub in duddingston village at the foot of the hill, with a claim to being scotland's oldest — full kitchen and a victorian skittle alley in the back.
alt: bross bagels — montreal-style bagels in portobello. →
portobello [beach] →
edinburgh's seaside suburb — two miles of sand, a victorian promenade, and cafés facing the firth.
the regent, abbeyhill [queer] →
gay-owned pub on montrose terrace — cask ales, dogs, board games, no dance floor.
eleanore [dinner] →
small-plates restaurant on leith walk — counter seating, natural wine, a short daily menu. tiny; book ahead.
alt: the walnut — neighbourhood bistro on the walk, also small, also books out. →
nightcap: carriers quarters — bernard street, leith's oldest pub (1785), trad sessions some nights. →

thu 09 — old town, west port, film at the cameo

start early at the top of the walk.

route on google maps ↗

twelve triangles [breakfast] →
leith walk bakery — doughnuts, sourdough, filled croissants, good coffee. the elm row shop is at the top of the walk.
alt: artisan roast — broughton street roastery, the city's original specialty coffee shop. →
calton hill [view] →
a twenty-minute climb to the national monument, nelson's tower, and a 360° view over the city, the firth, and arthur's seat.
royal mile → victoria street → grassmarket [walk] →
the old town sweep: the royal mile's closes and courts, the curved victoria street terraces, and the grassmarket below the castle rock.
st giles' cathedral [church] →
the high kirk of edinburgh, mid-royal-mile, under the crown spire — free entry, and the thistle chapel is a small gothic masterpiece (small donation).
oink [lunch] →
hog roast rolls from a counter on victoria street — one thing, done since 2008.
alt: union of genius — soup bar near the museum. →
treat: mary's milk bar — grassmarket gelato made fresh each morning. →
the bow bar [pub] →
single-room free house on victoria street — eight casks, some 400 whiskies, no music, no tv.
greyfriars kirkyard [walk] →
17th-century kirkyard off the grassmarket — mortsafes, covenanters' prison, four centuries of headstones. free, two minutes from the bookshops.
armchair books [used books] →
secondhand shop on west port with floor-to-ceiling stacks and a narrow path between them.
alt: edinburgh books — next door, tidier, equally deep. →
also: old town bookshop — victoria street, antiquarian stock, old maps and prints. →
surgeons' hall museums [museum] →
the royal college of surgeons' collections on nicolson street — pathology specimens, surgical history, and the burke & hare death mask. ~£10, closes 5pm.
alt (rain): national museum of scotland — free and vast, five minutes from the grassmarket. →
main point books [used books] →
secondhand bookshop on the bread street corner — fiction, poetry, and oddities.
alt: tills bookshop — hope park crescent by the meadows, secondhand since 1985. →
hey palu [martini] →
italian aperitivo bar on bread street — martinis, negronis, amari, seats at the bar. seven minutes from the cameo.
ting thai caravan [dinner] →
canteen-style thai in tollcross — shared tables, quick kitchen, big flavours.
alt: kim's mini meals — tiny family-run korean on buccleuch street. cash. →
the cameo — the furious [film · book thu showing] →
independent cinema in tollcross, screening since 1914 — ornate main auditorium and a bar in the lobby. confirm thursday's showtime and book at picturehouses.com.
alt: filmhouse — lothian road, three screens of repertory and new arthouse. filmhouse.org.uk. →
sandy bell's [music] →
one-room pub on forrest road with a nightly trad/folk session in the corner.
alt: captain's bar — south college street, smaller still, music most nights. →
or: the 22 bus runs down the walk.
