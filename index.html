edinburgh, on foot

tue 07 — thu 09 july 2026. base: a flat in leith, off the walk.
bookshops, one arthouse screen, queer pubs not clubs.

tue 07 — arrival day: new town, stockbridge, the walk home

in from dundee, early. bag → waverley left luggage (nr. platform 2), or ask lucy about an early drop. order below is the walking order.

route on google maps ↗

the milkman [coffee] →
tiny specialty coffee on cockburn street, two minutes from waverley. flat white and a pastry while the galleries open.
alt: fortitude coffee — york place, on the line north.
dundas street galleries [art] →
the scottish gallery and open eye, anchors of the new town art strip. both open 10am — coffee first.
shelter bookshop, stockbridge [used books] →
dedicated charity bookshop on raeburn place with a genuinely good secondhand stock. stockbridge's other charity shops are famously strong too.
kay's bar [pub] →
tiny former wine merchant's on india street — coal fire, cask ale, regulars. the daytime pint.
evening option: the last word saloon — candlelit stockbridge basement, serious classic martinis, if you're back this way after dark.
the pantry [lunch] →
stockbridge brunch-lunch staple, easy solo, proper cooking.
alt: söderberg — swedish bakery, cardamom buns and a quick bench lunch.
dean village [walk] →
fifteen minutes upstream on the water of leith path. absurdly picturesque old milling village — go before the tour groups.
royal botanic garden [garden] →
free. a slow hour, best castle skyline from the lawns.
scottish national portrait gallery [museum] →
neo-gothic red sandstone hall on queen street. scotland told through faces. free, on the line back toward broughton.
the street [queer] →
first pint in the pink triangle, broughton street's historic gay quarter. bar, not club.
alt: planet — a block away, same energy.
mcnaughtan's bookshop [used books] →
scotland's oldest antiquarian dealer, directly on the walk down to the flat. basically the local.
elvis shakespeare [used books · records] →
secondhand books and vinyl under one roof at 347 leith walk — punk singles to penguin classics. steps from the barber.
mo's sharp style [barber] →
280 leith walk, on the way down to the flat. buzz and beard trim, walk-in, open till 7 — reviews rave about the beard work specifically.
alt: blank. barbershop — stockbridge, if you'd rather do it in the morning. small, book ahead.
nauticus [martini] →
duke street's neighbourhood cocktail bar — award-level classics served without ceremony, locals at the bar. the pre-dinner martini.
alt: eleanore — counter seat, natural wine, small plates. book if energy holds.
fishers leith [dinner] →
the shore's seafood institution since 1991, in an old windmill tower — and you can eat at the bar. solo-perfect.
alt: the king's wark — 500-year-old stone pub, seriously good food.
port o' leith bar [pub] →
the legendary hyper-local on constitution street — nautical clutter, regulars, no polish whatsoever. leith in one room.
alt: roseleaf — cosier, teapot cocktails.

wed 08 — old town early, books at west port, film at the cameo

tourist hours beaten by starting at the top of the walk.

route on google maps ↗

twelve triangles [breakfast] →
leith walk's own bakery — doughnuts, sourdough, good coffee. the elm row shop is on the way up to calton hill.
alt: artisan roast — broughton street, the city's original specialty roaster.
calton hill [view] →
25 min from the flat, 20 up. the best view in the city — go before 9, before the crowds.
royal mile → victoria street → grassmarket [walk] →
the honest tourist sweep, done early enough to enjoy it.
oink [lunch] →
hog roast rolls on victoria street. counter, casual, built for eating alone standing up.
alt: union of genius — soup bar near the museum.
treat: mary's milk bar — grassmarket gelato, the queue is worth it.
the bow bar [pub] →
victoria street's serious little free house — cask ale, ~400 whiskies, no music, no tv, bar stools. the midday pint done right.
armchair books [used books] →
west port. chaotic, towering secondhand stacks. correct.
alt: edinburgh books — next door, tidier, equally deep.
also: old town bookshop — victoria street, antiquarian, old maps and prints.
surgeons' hall museums [museum] →
pathology specimens, the burke & hare death mask, real medical-history weirdness. ~£10, closes 5pm, nicolson st.
alt (rain): national museum of scotland — free, vast, five minutes from the grassmarket.
main point books [used books] →
secondhand shop on the bread street corner, two minutes from hey palu — a browse while the martini hour approaches.
alt: tills bookshop — hope park crescent by the meadows, classic secondhand, on a pleasant detour from surgeons' hall.
hey palu [martini] →
italian aperitivo bar on bread street, seven minutes from the cameo. bar seats, and among the best classic martinis and negronis in the city. the pre-dinner drink.
ting thai caravan [dinner] →
counter-style thai in tollcross, fast and loud in the good way. pre-film dinner.
alt: kim's mini meals — tiny korean, cult following, cash.
the cameo — the furious, 8:15pm [film · to book] →
1914 auditorium, proper bar for the pre-film pint. picturehouses.com.
alt: filmhouse — lothian rd, 10 min away, full programme at filmhouse.org.uk.
sandy bell's [music] →
nightly trad/folk session on forrest road, roughly on the walk back. tiny, loud, the real thing — squeeze in for one.
alt: captain's bar — even tinier, south college st, music most nights.
alt: skip it — a bus down the walk is honourable.

thu 09 — arthur's seat to the sea, and a leith finale

the trip's summit day. gentle line up and down — left side gets a vote.

route on google maps ↗

williams & johnson [coffee] →
leith roastery in custom lane — the proper flat white before the climb.
alt: twelve triangles, duke street — pastry ballast for the hill.
arthur's seat via dunsapie loch [hike] →
the gradual route both directions; skip the gutted haddie scramble. short summit push, whole city and firth below.
sheep heid inn, duddingston [lunch] →
arguably scotland's oldest pub, at the bottom of the descent. proper food too — post-summit lunch and a half are compulsory. skittle alley in the back.
alt: bross bagels — portobello's montreal-style bagel shop, if you'd rather eat by the sea.
portobello [beach] →
walk east to the beach suburb. promenade, coffee, sea air.
the regent, abbeyhill [queer] →
the queer pub done right — cask ale, dogs, zero club energy. on the walk back.
eleanore [dinner] →
last supper in leith — counter seating, natural wine, inventive small plates. tiny, book ahead.
alt: the walnut — neighbourhood bistro on the walk, also tiny, also book.
nightcap: carriers quarters — leith's oldest pub (1785), bernard st; trad sessions some nights, worth checking.

fri 10 — loose end day, pack, resupply. sat 11 — fly home from edi.
