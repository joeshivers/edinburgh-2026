edinburgh, on foot

tue 07 — thu 09 july 2026. base: a flat in leith, off the walk.
bookshops, one arthouse screen, queer pubs not clubs.

tue 07 — arrival day: new town, stockbridge, the walk home

in from dundee, early. bag → waverley left luggage (nr. platform 2), or ask lucy about an early drop. order below is the walking order.

dundas street galleries
the scottish gallery and open eye, anchors of the new town art strip. both open 10am — coffee first.
golden hare books [books]
stockbridge's small, perfectly-curated indie. the neighbourhood's charity shops are famously good too.
dean village
fifteen minutes upstream on the water of leith path. absurdly picturesque old milling village — go before the tour groups.
royal botanic garden
free. a slow hour, best castle skyline from the lawns.
scottish national portrait gallery [museum]
neo-gothic red sandstone hall on queen street. scotland told through faces. free, on the line back toward broughton.
the street [queer]
first pint in the pink triangle, broughton street's historic gay quarter. bar, not club.
alt: planet — a block away, same energy.
mcnaughtan's bookshop [books]
scotland's oldest antiquarian dealer, directly on the walk down to the flat. basically the local.
roseleaf
pints on the shore — cosy, teapot cocktails, zero pretension.
alt: the king's wark — 500-year-old stone pub on the water.
alt: eleanore — counter seat, natural wine, small plates. book if energy holds.

wed 08 — old town early, books at west port, film at the cameo

tourist hours beaten by starting at the top of the walk.

calton hill
25 min from the flat, 20 up. the best view in the city — go before 9, before the crowds.
royal mile → victoria street → grassmarket
the honest tourist sweep, done early enough to enjoy it.
armchair books [books]
west port. chaotic, towering, correct.
alt: edinburgh books — next door, tidier, equally deep.
surgeons' hall museums [museum]
pathology specimens, the burke & hare death mask, real medical-history weirdness. ~£10, closes 5pm, nicolson st.
alt (rain): national museum of scotland — free, vast, five minutes from the grassmarket.
ting thai caravan
counter-style thai in tollcross, fast and loud in the good way. pre-film dinner.
alt: kim's mini meals — tiny korean, cult following, cash.
the cameo — the furious, 8:15pm [to book]
1914 auditorium, proper bar for the pre-film pint. picturehouses.com.
alt: filmhouse — lothian rd, 10 min away, full programme at filmhouse.org.uk.
walk home via broughton
optional pink triangle nightcap; otherwise a bus down the walk is honourable.

thu 09 — arthur's seat to the sea, and a leith finale

the trip's summit day. gentle line up and down — left side gets a vote.

arthur's seat via dunsapie loch
the gradual route both directions; skip the gutted haddie scramble. short summit push, whole city and firth below.
sheep heid inn, duddingston
arguably scotland's oldest pub, at the bottom of the descent. a half at minimum is compulsory.
portobello
walk east to the beach suburb. promenade, coffee, sea air.
the regent, abbeyhill [queer]
the queer pub done right — cask ale, dogs, zero club energy. on the walk back.
eleanore
last supper in leith — counter seating, natural wine, inventive small plates. tiny, book ahead.
alt: the walnut — neighbourhood bistro on the walk, also tiny, also book.

fri 10 — loose end day, pack, resupply. sat 11 — fly home from edi.
